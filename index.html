Global Relationships Through Media:

A Study of the United States Using ICEWS

Concept

The global interactions of governments, ethnic groups, world leaders, and ideas in general is a complex network made up of a collection of events and exchanges of sentiment. The Integrated Crisis Early Warning System (ICEWS) was developed to help understand these relationships and provide a comprehensive field of data that can be collected, manipulated and analyzed to help model the events and connections throughout the world. ICEWS is a collection of news reports that are compiled through Natural Language Processing algorithms that structure and parse the key attributes of media reporting.

Tools and Resources

The ICEWS database consists of several individual datasets that must be collected and compiled if the analysis requires a temporal focus spanning multiple years. For this project, a total collection of data from 2012-2019 consists of over 5 million records. Visualizations are being produced using three different resources: Google, Kepler, and Tableau.

ICEWS

Originally develope as part of the Harvard Dataverse, ICEWS has historical datasets that date back into 20th Century and recently began releasing daily event datasets in late 2018.
